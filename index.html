Hyperspectral Structure and Algorithm Design of Marine Ship Monitoring Micro/Nano Satellite

The project's hyperspectral imager, a camera that can fit into a small satellite, can take continuous images of the same area to track ships in the area and observe their traffic, collect research on illegal fishing or drug traffic, and also has practical implications for identifying ships, assisting in tracking targets, and providing other supporting information to avoid collisions.

I was responsible for designing the algorithm in MATLAB, which is used to identify the ships in the image and calculate the size, heading, and speed of the ship. I used real data to test the algorithm and the test file generated in real data format.

Shake-down analysis of bi-modulus structures

In this paper, the relationship between stress and displacement of a three-bar structure with a bi-modulus of elasticity in tension and compression is investigated by using the elastic-plasticity theory, and an expression for the ultimate external load is derived for a three-bar structure with a bi-modulus of elasticity in tension and compression to maintain stability. Further calculations are carried out to analyze the different tensile yield stresses and compressive yield stresses. The calculation results show that the different modulus of elasticity in tension and compression and the asymmetric yield stress in tension and compression affect the ultimate external load of the structure, which is a safety issue of concern for engineering structures.
